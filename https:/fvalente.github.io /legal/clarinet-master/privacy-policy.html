Clarinet Master — Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 24, 2026

Clarinet Master is a practice companion for clarinet players. We respect your privacy. This policy explains what data the app accesses, why, and how it's handled.

What we access

Microphone. Clarinet Master uses your phone's microphone to detect the pitch of the notes you play during practice, drone, tuner, calibration, sheet music, interactive fingering chart, call-and-response, and recording sessions. Audio is processed entirely on your device in real time. No audio recording is ever uploaded, transmitted, or stored on any server. The microphone is only active while you have a practice screen open.

Recordings. When you tap Record in the Recording screen, a WAV file is saved to the app's private storage on your device. Recordings stay on your phone — you can rename, share, or delete them from the recording list, and clear them in bulk from Settings → Storage. They never leave your device unless you explicitly share one via the system share sheet.

App-usage and crash data (Firebase). We use Google Firebase Analytics and Crashlytics to understand which features are used and to receive automatic crash reports when the app misbehaves. This data is anonymous and aggregated — we do not collect names, email addresses, contact lists, location, contacts, photos, or any personal identifiers. Firebase's data handling is governed by Google's privacy policy.

Practice history. Your exercise scores, daily usage minutes, badges, Learning Journey progress, and imported MusicXML pieces are stored locally on your device using Android SharedPreferences. They never leave your phone.

Purchases. If you upgrade to Clarinet Master Pro, the purchase is handled entirely by Google Play Billing. We never see your credit card or payment details — Google does. We only learn that "this device owns the Pro entitlement" so the free-tier limits are removed.

What we do NOT collect

  • Audio recordings of your playing.
  • Your name, email, or contact information.
  • Your location.
  • Your contacts, photos, calendar, or any other device data.
  • Any information that could personally identify you.

Microphone permission

Android will ask for microphone permission the first time you open a screen that needs it. You can revoke it at any time in Settings → Apps → Clarinet Master → Permissions. Without microphone access, the practice, tuner, drone, calibration, and recording screens will display a "Microphone is off — tap to enable" state, but the app will not crash and the rest of the app will continue to work.

Notifications

If you turn on the daily practice reminder in Settings → Reminders, Android will ask for notification permission. The reminder is scheduled locally on your device — no push servers are involved. You can turn it off at any time in the same settings screen.

Children's privacy

Clarinet Master is intended for clarinet players aged 13 and above. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

If we update this policy, we'll change the "Last updated" date above and surface a notice in the app on the next launch.
